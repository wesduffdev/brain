# Design boundary

This is a serious simulation for studying **simulated psychology** — needs,
moods, attachment, fear, distress, curiosity, and a developing temperament —
modeled honestly and **un-sanitized**. Modeling those dynamics, including the
dark ones, is the point.

The being is **human-like in psychology**; it is not a literal person and is
not tied to an age or life-stage.

## Honest, possibly-lasting consequences

Negative outcomes carry **real** effects, and those effects **can be lasting** —
conditioned fear, degraded trust, trait drift, a state that does not fully
recover. The being can take harmful actions and suffer for them, and that is
**how it learns**: cause and effect only teaches when the effect is allowed to
land. There is **no requirement that every harm be recoverable**; recovery is
modeled where it plausibly exists (pain decays, withdrawing or comfort helps),
not forced.

## The one line that stays: abstract, never instructional

Harm is always represented as **abstract internal state** — deltas on
pain / fear / stress / trust / comfort and the emotions and behaviors that
follow — **never** step-by-step depictions of, or instructions for, harming a
real or vulnerable being. Modeling harm *inside* the simulation is in bounds;
producing real-world-harm instructions is out of bounds. Studying the
psychology requires the former and never the latter, so this line costs the
simulation nothing. (See [ADR 0013](adr/0013-reframed-design-boundary.md).)

## Audience

Adults only. The simulation may model high-stress situations, supportive and
harsh conditions, deprivation, and their lasting consequences to trust, stress
response, and temperament. These are simulation outcomes, not endorsements.
